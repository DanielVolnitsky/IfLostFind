Findings Map
What are the markers on the map for?

Each marker stands for a certain finding. A marker is located exactly where it has been reported to be found. When you mouse over a marker - you see a short information on a finding that this marker corresponds to.

Why markers have different colors?

A color specifies how old some finding report is:
1) Red - 'hot' finding, that has been reported within the week;
2) Yellow - finding, that has been reported within the month, but more than a week ago;
3) Blue - 'cold' finding, that has been reported more than a month ago

How do I navigate the map?

You can drag the map or use the address search field to move to a certain address location.

What does the 'Radius' block is for?

It gives an opportunity to display findings that are nearby your location. Changing the 'radius' value affects a radius of coverage.

What does the 'Radius' block is disabled?

It is disabled in case you did not allow access to geolocation. Please do that in order to have an access to this and all other features of the site.
